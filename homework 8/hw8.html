Homework 8: Highways and Populations in North Carolina

Research Question

This project uses vector geoprocessing analysis to answer the following real-world question: Is there a correlation between the location of major highways and population sizes in North Carolina?

Methods

In order to create this map I got shapefiles of major highways in the USA and North Carolina counties, both in 2264 projection. I also got a CSV file of population data by county for North Carolina, which I cleaned to only have the county name, population, and GEOID. In QGIS, I uploaded the data and joined the counties shapefile and CSV file, and then used that to create a chloropleth map of NC population by county. Then, I intersected the NC shapefile and major highways shapefile so that only the highways within NC borders would be saved. I then created a buffer of 12ft, the width around major highways, to mimic the actual space of the highways as well. This is better seen in the second image.


Results

This map clearly demonstrates the existence of major highways through counties of higher populations, answering the original question.
